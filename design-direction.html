What's different in v3

Background temperature
#18150f — warm espresso, not cold black
The background has a brown/amber bias throughout (#18150f → #1f1c14 → #26221a). Every element on the screen inherits warmth from it. Cold blacks feel clinical; this feels like a room at night with a lamp on. The parchment text (#e8ddd0) reinforces the warmth — it reads as paper, not a screen.
Recording button
Stays amber — no red alarm
The button stays warm amber (#c8955a) throughout the recording. State is communicated by: icon swap (mic → rounded stop square), thin ripple rings that expand and fade like water, animated waveform bars appearing above, and a live timer. The recording should feel like being listened to — not like an active alarm.
Ripple ring
1px border ring, not a glow blob
The animation during recording is a thin 1px border ring that scales from 1× to 1.55× and fades out — like a ripple on water. Two rings offset by 0.95s create a gentle pulse. This says "active" without any urgency or AI-orb association.
Typography weight
Cormorant 300 throughout — lighter than v2
Dropping from weight 400 to 300 throughout makes everything feel softer and less structured. The reflection body in italic 300 at 14.5px / lh 1.85 reads like a diary page — exactly the right register for this app.
Interactive
Full working prototype
Record button starts a live timer and shows the recording state with animated waveform. Tapping again goes to the reflection screen. Tab bar, mode picker, goal selector — all clickable. Use it before deciding on any code change.
